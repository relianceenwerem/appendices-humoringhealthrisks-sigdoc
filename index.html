Appendix A — Gabin's Theoretical Model

Table 1: Gabin's theoretical model adapted to the context of tactical risk communication

Adapted to the context of this study:

  • Point A — Symbol The social media post, infographic, video, or campaign.
  • Point C — Referent The body, the behavior, the condition being discussed.
  • Point B — Inverted Image The health risk reality (what is actually happening to the body, what the disease does, the statistical likelihood of harm).
  • Point D — Expected Image The audience's prior assumption (that they are healthy, that the risk does not apply to them, that the behavior is harmless).
  • Audience (center) The patient or public simultaneously holding their comfortable assumption (D) against the disrupting health reality (B).

Appendix C — Creator Profiles

Dr. Idrees Mughal

@dr_idz — Instagram

Dr. Idrees Mughal is an Instagram-verified and popular health content creator. A visit to his page establishes his credentials as a UK-trained Medical Doctor with a master's degree in Nutritional Research as well as a Board-Certified Lifestyle Medicine professional. His profile picture portrays him in a lab coat and stethoscope, typical of medical professionals. These are tactics used to establish credibility on this platform, outside the institution where he practices professionally. With over 700,000 Instagram followers, Dr. Idz communicates health risks by debunking health myths and countering misinformation using affordances like hashtags, short video content, and trends.

Dr. Franziska Haydanek

@pagingdrfran — Instagram

Dr. Franziska Haydanek explicitly positions herself as a board-certified obstetrician-gynecologist (ObGyn), and this professional identity is consistently foregrounded through her social media profiles. Her Instagram bio emphasizes both institutional credibility ("board-certified OBGYN") and experiential authority ("mother of three"), while the use of the female doctor and pregnant person emojis functions as a semiotic shorthand for reproductive expertise and care. The use of Instagram's highlight features to organize content into categories such as "PCOS," "PMDD," "breastfeeding," and "elective induction of labor" constructs a curated knowledge archive, signaling sustained engagement with women's health education. All of this shapes how viewers interpret her content, as they are already positioned to see her as a reliable source.

Dr. Adefunke Arowolo

@doctor.adefunke — Instagram

Dr. Adefunke Arowolo is a medical doctor, health advocate, and digital health content creator who leverages social media to deliver accessible health education through humor and storytelling. With over 280,000 followers on Instagram, she leverages details like "Medical Doctor," "Educative Medical Content," and "Spreading Awareness, One Laugh at a Time" in her bio to establish her professional credibility and content niche. Her content spans issues related to sexual and reproductive health, medication safety, food risks, mental health, and general wellness, often presented through short, humorous skits that integrate visual, verbal, and performative elements.

Skit Selection Criteria

Following creator selection, a sample of three skits was collected. The selection was based on the criteria that:

  • It was a short video that employed skit-style conventions;
  • It communicated a health risk or debunked a health misconception;
  • It was among the creator's 20 most recent posts at the time of data collection; and
  • It employed multimodality as a communicative strategy.

Each video was subsequently treated as an individual unit of analysis for the multimodal discourse analysis (MDA), while its humorous element was analyzed using Gabin's (1987) humor model.
